Client Self-Service Troubleshooting

A guide to diagnose and resolve common technical issues quickly.

Following this checklist often resolves incidents faster and provides our support team with critical information if escalation is necessary.

1. General Triage: The Essentials

Step 1: Check System Status & Refresh
ActionDetailWhat to Look For
Refresh/Restart Reload the application, web page, or if needed, restart your device (computer or mobile). Does the error persist after a clean restart?
Verify Connectivity Ensure you have a stable internet connection. Try loading an external site (e.g., Google). Is your connection speed slow or intermittent?
Check Status Pages Look for any known incidents on our official **System Status Page** (link provided in your onboarding materials). Is the outage known and are we already working on a fix?
Step 2: Test User & Permissions
ActionDetailWhat to Look For
Log Out and Back In Log out of the application completely and clear your session data before logging back in. Did the login/authentication process complete normally?
Test Another User If possible, have a colleague with different permissions try to replicate the issue. Is the problem specific to your user account, or system-wide?
Confirm Permissions Ensure you have the necessary **role or access level** to perform the action you are attempting. Did your permissions recently change?

2. Web Application & Browser Issues

Step 3: Clear Browser Cache & Cookies
ActionDetailWhy This Helps
Incognito/Private Mode Open the application in an Incognito (Chrome/Edge) or Private (Firefox/Safari) window. This bypasses cached files and most extensions, testing a clean session.
Hard Refresh (Bypass Cache) Use `Ctrl + Shift + R` (Windows/Linux) or `Cmd + Shift + R` (Mac). Forces the browser to download fresh copies of all files, ignoring old versions.
Clear Browser Data Go to your browser settings and **clear cookies and cached images/files** for the last 24 hours. Resolves issues caused by corrupted or outdated local files.
Step 4: Inspect the Console for Errors
ActionDetailCritical Information to Provide
Open Developer Tools Right-click anywhere on the page and select **Inspect**, then go to the **Console** tab. **Red Error Messages:** Note the text and line numbers for any JavaScript errors.
Check Network Activity Go to the **Network** tab, reload the page, and look for any failed requests (status codes like `401`, `404`, or `500`). Identify the **specific URL** that failed and its **HTTP Status Code**.

3. Connectivity & Environment Issues

Step 5: Validate Environment Configuration
ActionDetailWhat to Confirm
VPN/Firewall Status If you are using a VPN or internal network, ensure it is connected and configured correctly. Is your current IP address (or the server's IP) whitelisted by the network rules?
Check Endpoints If integrating with an API, ensure you are hitting the correct **API Endpoint** (e.g., `staging` vs. `production`). Is the issue only present in a specific environment (Dev, QA, or Prod)?
Review Usage Limits Check if your service usage (API calls, data storage) is nearing or exceeding its monthly or daily limit. Could the system be throttling your requests due to overuse?
Step 6: Documentation and Reporting
ActionDetailWhen Escalating, Include This:
Detailed Steps to Reproduce List the exact actions you took, click by click, to encounter the error. "1. Log in. 2. Click the 'Reports' tab. 3. Select 'Q1 Data'. **Expected:** Chart loads. **Actual:** Blank screen."
Screenshots/Video Capture screenshots of the error message, the **Browser Console**, or a short video demonstrating the failure. Visual evidence is extremely helpful for rapid diagnosis.
Time and Location Note the exact **date and time (including timezone)** the error occurred, and your general **location** (e.g., "Chicago, IL"). Helps correlate with server logs and geo-specific performance issues.

If the Issue Persists...

If you have completed all relevant steps above and the issue remains unresolved, please create a new support ticket.

**MANDATORY:** When escalating, include **all the information gathered** during this self-service process (error messages, status codes, and the detailed steps to reproduce).
