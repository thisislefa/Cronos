Technology has always promised us more—more speed, more access, more connection. And, in a way, it’s delivered. We live in a world where entire careers are built in cloud spaces, friendships are formed in comment sections, and secrets are kept (or lost) behind encryption keys. We’re more connected than ever. But that connection comes with strings—ones most people never see until they’re tangled in them. I didn’t get into cybersecurity because it sounded cool. I got into it because I noticed something: the smarter our tech got, the more blind spots it created. The convenience of it all started to quietly invite risk, and the average person? They were too busy living their lives to notice. They trusted the system. The passwords. The networks. The apps. They didn’t know where their data went after they clicked “Agree.” They didn’t know how easily things could be manipulated, intercepted, or lost. And let’s be honest—many still don’t.
